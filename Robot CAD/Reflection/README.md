# Reflection
| Name | Class | Status |
|------|-------|--------|
|Reflection      |Antweight       |0-2       |

Reflection is a full combat antweight designed primarily as a test platform. V2 has fought and went 0-2. V3 has never fought. V1 is not released.

V3 Onshape: https://cad.onshape.com/documents/77637ca4f752c54e35cf089e/w/12f24f7fb4df0769d7ef96ab/e/62a3ec6d1df3cc2ba4c8ca14

# Fab

- 2x Armor Panel - 1mm Gr5 Ti
- 1x Left Block - TPU
- 1x Right Block - TPU
- 1x Ear - 2mm 7075

- 1x MagDock - PLA

- 1x Motor Mount Right (repeat or spark) - TPU

# Parts
- 2x Fingertech silver spark OR 2x Repeat MK2 motors
- 1x 2207 brushless motor
- 1x 380mAh GNB 3S battery
- 2x Fingertech 0.75 twist hubs OR Repeat eco hubs
- 2x 0.75x1.75" foam wheels (the ones on the Scalar kit)
- 1x Brushless opto ESC
- 1x Dual brushed ESC (I used SDMD MK1)
- 1x Receiver (I used RF nano)
- 1x XT30 connector
- 1x Fingertech switch
- 8x M5x10 bolts
- 8x M5x10 nuts
- 8x M5 lock nuts
- 1x MF105ZZ bearing
- 14x M3x6 bolts
- 8x M3x8 bolts
- 18x M3 nuts
- 6x M3 plastite screws

# Assembly 
1. Use 4x M3x6 bolts and nuts to secure 2x top motor mounts to the mounts on the lid.
2. Bolt uprights in place using 4x M5x10 bolts and nuts. The bolt face should be inside the chassis.
3. Bolt side armor pieces in place using 4x M5x10 bolts and nuts. The bolt face should be inside the armor piece.
4. Bolt magdock into bottom of chassis using 2x M3x6 bolts and nuts.
5. Stack up the weapon, weapon hub, and washer. Thread 4x M3x10 bolts through the holes and secure the assembly with 4 M3 nuts.
6. Assemble wiring harness, including all motors and XT30 connector. Leave FT switch loose.
7. Slip motors into the L and R mounts, then press the mounts into the slots in the chassis.
8. Slide weapon assembly onto 2207 motor shaft. Secure with the motor's included lock nut.
9. Bolt 2207 to the left upright (left when viewed from the back) using 4x M3x10 bolts.
10. Press MF105ZZ bearing into the right upright, supporting the end of the 2207.
11. Use 8x M3x6 bolts and nuts to secure both titanium armor plates to the front of the chassis.
12. Connect connect battery to robot. Now that you have a good idea of how everything sits in the bot, find a free space for your FT switch and super glue it there. Unplug battery.
13. Attach wheel hub body to motor shafts, slide tire on, then attach outside of hub body.
14. Secure lid using 6x M3 plastite screws. The top motor mounts from the first step should clamp down on the motors, enclosing them safely.
